About this app

  • Instructions:

    • To move to the next section, press "a".

    To move to the previous section, press "b".

    To move to the section one item up, press "c".

    To move to the section one item down, press "d".

    To click the selected button, press "e".

  • Test description:

    • I tested with two different parts of my body - the knuckle of a curled index finger and my nose. 3 tests each. Equations: 4*5-2, 90/9, 87+3 (includes = and C for all equations)

  • Test results:

    • Average time for finger = 1 min 10 sec

    Average time for nose = 1 min 12 sec

    # of errors for finger = 1

    # of errors for nose = 0

  • Discussion:

    • The time difference between the two methods was a lot less than I thought it would be.

    One possibility is that I knew the system of buttons better by the second time around.

    I also expected more errors with the nose method - though I noted that both methods would have been much easier with larger buttons than the keys on my (relatively small) keyboard.

    Overall, a 5-switch method seems to work surprisingly well, and though using it through keyboard keys may be difficult for someone with a mobility impairment, the implementation of a different set of switches could easily fix that issue.
